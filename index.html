Pop-culture got some of it wrong!

Their boats were technologically unrivalled in their age. They were fast, nimble, and commanded by some of the best naval capable people of the era.

Most vikings were actually farmers first and foremost. Going "viking" was secondary to their home lives.

Vikings did not have horned helms (probably). This was a legend originating by artists portraying Vikings with horned helmets, hundreds of years after the Viking era.

Norse religion (as we know it today) is based on context and assumptions. The belief systems were not written down, rather they were passed down by word of mouth. All we have are accounts by some Christians and historical context to go on.

-Volsunga Saga, c.21
